Arsenal FC History

Arsenal Football Club was established in 1886 by a group of workers at the Woolwich Arsenal. Originally named "Dial Square," the club later became "Royal Arsenal" and finally "Arsenal."

Arsenal achieved significant success under legendary manager Herbert Chapman in the 1930s, introducing innovative tactics and leading the club to multiple league titles.

One of the club's greatest achievements came in the 2003-2004 season when Arsenal went unbeaten in the league, earning the nickname "The Invincibles."
